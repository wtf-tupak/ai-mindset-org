A framework

The Art of Knowledge Management

capture, connect, create

A practical philosophy for building a personal knowledge system that works with you — not against you.

The Problem

What we lose every day

  • Context collapses between meetings — the "why" disappears before the "what" is even finished.
  • Ideas evaporate between the spark and the page — the best thought you had vanished on the commute.
  • Connections never form — two ideas that should have met live in different apps, unseen.

Three Principles

The foundation of a working system

Capture
Note everything that surprises you — friction is the enemy of the fleeting idea.
Connect
Link ideas across time and context — knowledge compounds at the edge of categories.
Create
Produce from your knowledge — a note that never becomes something is just stored anxiety.

Architecture

"Your second brain is not a storage — it's a thinking partner."

— Tiago Forte
  • Design your system around retrieval, not storage — the question is never "where did I put it?" but "when will I need it?"
  • Let the structure emerge from use — a perfect taxonomy built upfront will collapse under the weight of real thinking.

Daily Practice

Rituals that make the system real

01
Morning Review
Scan yesterday's captures. Promote what still matters. Delete what no longer does. Five minutes builds weeks of clarity.
02
Active Capture
One trusted inbox. No decisions during capture — that's processing, not collecting. Context is king: record why alongside what.
03
Evening Reflection
One connection per day. Link a new note to an old one. The habit is small; the effect is cumulative and irreversible.

Structure

One vault, layered by purpose

A simple folder hierarchy that mirrors how knowledge actually moves — from raw to refined.

# Obsidian vault — minimal viable structure

inbox/          # raw captures — untouched, unsorted
notes/          # processed — linked, tagged, permanent
projects/       # active work — temporary by design
  └── MOC.md    # map of content — the connective tissue
archive/        # finished — searchable, not browsable

The tool matters less than the habit. Any system you maintain consistently outperforms a perfect system you abandon.
